Story of the Ganga River: Its Pollution and Rejuvenation

9sep 2024

River Ganga is one of the most important and sacred rivers in India

Water is indispensable for the basic subsistence of human beings. It makes sense that most civilizations have emerged along riverbanks or in river basins throughout the world. India is fortunate to have a large number of rivers in this aspect. The holiest, revered, and significant river in North India is the Ganga , the country's national river. 44% of India's population depends on it, and it comes from the Gangotri glacier in Uttarakhand. The riverine water resources support the aquatic ecology and provide irrigation, drinking water, energy, cost-effective transportation, recreation, and religious fulfilment for a large number of stakeholders.The stories and myths about the river and how it interacts with people and the environment have been around for a very long time. Due to the building of dams and barrages, the discharge of untreated municipal and industrial waste, floral offerings, and the cremation of dead people on its bank, Ganga is now significantly reduced in flow and completely polluted. Both inorganic and organic toxins from industrial waste, agricultural runoff, and religious rituals are the main sources of pollution in the Ganga River. With varying degrees of success, the Government of India (GOI) has tried numerous measures to clean up the Ganga over the years, including the Ganga More than 400 million people in India and Bangladesh live in and depend on the basin, which has a rich cultural and religious history. It is the source of light for human civilization. People have a great deal of faith in Ganga's abilities to heal and regenerate. It is without a doubt the most sacred river in India, and both locals and visitors to the country hold it in the highest regard. Due to the year- round abundance of water, which makes up 25% of India's water resources, Ganga has significantly contributed to the development of Indian civilization and the economy (Paul et al., 2013). India's most revered river, Ganga, is personified as a goddess and treated with motherly respect. It is thought to be a soul cleanser and has been mentioned as having therapeutic and antibacterial qualities (Nautiyal, 2009; Rai, 2013). Today, however, habitual ingestion of Ganga water or bathing in it run the risk of catastrophic health consequences, including cancer.

Biographies

  • Ganga River is the second largest river in the Indian subcontinent by water flow.
  • It flows through India and Bangladesh where it is called as River Padma.
  • This river got its name after the Hindu goddess Ganga and it is considered very sacred.
  • Ganga flows from north India and falls into the Bay of Bengal in eastern India.
  • The length of the Ganga River is 3877 km where 2525 km flows in the Indian side.
  • When Ganga enters West Bengal, it is known as “Hooghly River” or “Bhagirathi-Hooghly”.
